QGIS Short Course

June 27, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Welcome!

New Maps Plus at the University of Kentucky Department of Geography is excited to offer this unique QGIS short course. Please check back here as we will post more details before the event. Boyd Shearer is the instructor and you should contact him with any questions.

What do you need to bring?

This workshop will provide the computer, data, and instructional material. You don't need any special software or device. However, you are welcome to bring your laptop, though we will NOT spend time installing QGIS and will focus only on the Windows version.

The course is at full capacity, but if time allows, you can share your data and questions at the end of the workshop. Your data should be downloadable or on a portable drive. The instructor will be available after class to discuss your data.

Location and parking

The workshop is held in the University of Kentucky White Hall Classroom Building rooms 311 and 313. The nearest visitor parking is available in Parking Structure #5, which is shown on the above map and in this Campus map. The rate is $2 per hour with a $16 maximum. Other visitor parking locations are shown on the map, too.

Parking alert!

We've been informed that UK will be hosting a freshman orientation on Tuesday and the Parking Structure #5 will likely be full by 8:30 am (and possibly free). Therefore, we recommend arriving on campus early to find parking.

Metered on-street parking is available, but not likely for the entire duration of the workshop. If you are willing to walk and the weather is nice, neighborhoods to the northeast don't charge for on-street parking. Campus neighborhoods often require parking permits, but you might get lucky and find a free spot.

Lunch and snacks

A break for lunch is between noon and 1 PM. Campus has a lot of great restaurants for you to find lunch. Snacks are available vending machines in the basement of White Hall

No food or open beverages are allowed at the computer stations. The lab has a classroom (shown in this 360° photo) where one can enjoy snacks.
